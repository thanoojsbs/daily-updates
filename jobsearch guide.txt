Below is a GitHub-ready guide written as an onboarding document for new Master's students searching for jobs in the USA.

# USA Job Search Playbook for Master's Students (OPT/STEM OPT)

## Purpose

This guide is designed for Master's students and recent graduates searching for Software Engineering, DevOps, Cloud, SRE, Platform Engineering, and related technology roles in the United States.

The goal is simple:

**Get interviews faster by applying smarter, not by applying to thousands of random jobs.**

---

# Before You Start

Most students fail because they:

* Apply everywhere without a strategy
* Search only on LinkedIn
* Ignore location-based hiring trends
* Apply days after jobs are posted
* Never contact recruiters
* Do not track applications

A professional job search is treated like a daily process, not an occasional activity.

---

# Rule #1: Search by Region, Not by USA

Do NOT search:

```text
Software Engineer - United States
```

Do search:

```text
Software Engineer - Dallas, TX
Software Engineer - Austin, TX
Software Engineer - Raleigh, NC
Software Engineer - Atlanta, GA
DevOps Engineer - Phoenix, AZ
Cloud Engineer - Denver, CO
```

### Recommended Regions

| Region      | Why                              |
| ----------- | -------------------------------- |
| Dallas, TX  | Large tech hiring market         |
| Austin, TX  | Startup + Enterprise hiring      |
| Raleigh, NC | Strong demand, lower competition |
| Atlanta, GA | Fortune 500 hiring               |
| Phoenix, AZ | Growing cloud market             |
| Denver, CO  | Strong DevOps/SRE demand         |
| Chicago, IL | Enterprise technology jobs       |
| Tampa, FL   | Lower competition                |

Avoid focusing only on:

* Silicon Valley
* San Francisco
* New York City

Competition is significantly higher.

---

# Rule #2: Fresh Jobs Win

Applications submitted early get noticed.

### Always Filter By

LinkedIn:

```text
Past 24 Hours
Most Recent
```

Preferred:

```text
Past Hour
```

### Application Priority

| Posted Time    | Priority |
| -------------- | -------- |
| Under 1 Hour   | Highest  |
| Under 24 Hours | High     |
| 1–3 Days       | Medium   |
| 7+ Days        | Low      |

Target:

```text
Apply within 30 minutes whenever possible.
```

# Rule #5: Check Sponsorship Before Applying

Do not waste applications.

Check whether the company has sponsored H1B visas before.

Use:

* H1BGrader
* MyVisaJobs
* USCIS H1B Employer Database

Check:

* Sponsorship history
* Number of approvals
* Job titles sponsored

---

# Rule #6: Use Advanced Search Queries

Instead of:

```text
DevOps
```

Use:

```text
("DevOps Engineer" OR "Cloud Engineer" OR "SRE")
AND AWS
```

Instead of:

```text
Java
```

Use:

```text
("Software Engineer" OR "Backend Engineer")
AND "Spring Boot"
```

This reveals jobs hidden from normal searches.

# Rule #7: Build a Daily Job Search Routine

### Morning Session

Duration: 30 Minutes

Tasks:

* LinkedIn recent jobs
* Company career pages
* New postings

---

### Afternoon Session

Duration: 30 Minutes

Tasks:

* Regional searches
* Visa-friendly companies
* Application tracking

---

### Evening Session

Duration: 30 Minutes

Tasks:

* Recruiter outreach
* Networking
* Referral requests

---

# Rule #8: Contact Recruiters

After applying:

1. Find recruiter.
2. Send connection request.
3. Follow up politely.

Template:

```text
Hello [Recruiter Name],

I recently applied for the [Position Name].

I am currently pursuing my Master's degree and have experience in [Skills].

I would appreciate any guidance regarding the opportunity.

Thank you.
```

Target:

```text
10 recruiter connections per day
```

---

# Rule #9: Track Everything

Create a spreadsheet.

| Company | Role | Location | Date Applied | Recruiter | Status |
| ------- | ---- | -------- | ------------ | --------- | ------ |

Status:

```text
Applied
Assessment
Interview
Rejected
Offer
```

Never rely on memory.

---

# Rule #10: Apply Quality Over Quantity

Bad:

```text
300 random applications
```

Good:

```text
20 targeted applications
10 recruiter messages
2 referrals
```

Quality applications produce more interviews.

# Professional Job Search Formula

```text
Fresh Jobs
+
Target Regions
+
Recruiter Outreach
+
Referrals
+
Consistent Tracking

More Interviews
```

---

# Final Advice

Treat job searching like a full-time project.

Do not measure success by:

```text
Number of Applications
```

Measure success by:

```text
Recruiter Responses
Assessments Received
Interview Calls
Referral Conversions
```

A candidate who applies strategically in the right regions with fresh postings and recruiter outreach will consistently outperform someone who blindly submits hundreds of applications.
